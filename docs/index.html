From the Arabic ḥisāb — "to reckon, to count." Over a thousand years ago, Arab mathematicians used it for ḥisāb al-hind, the Indian art of counting that grew into the numerals the whole world writes with today. The word has always meant one quiet thing: knowing where you stand.
